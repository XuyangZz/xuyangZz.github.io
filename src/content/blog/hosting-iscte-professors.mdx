<p class="caption-en"><span class="lang-tag">EN</span>Day one: I met the four professors at the airport arrivals hall with a sign that, in retrospect, I should have made bigger. <p class="caption-zh"><span class="lang-tag">中文</span>第一天：在机场到达大厅接机，举着一块牌子——事后觉得应该做得更大一点。扫了半个小时的脸，Ricardo 先挥手了，那种笑容五十米外都能认出来。从那一刻起，整趟行程的节奏跟我预想的不一样。</p>

## 校园参观 / Campus Tour

<div class="img-row cols-2">
  <img src="/portugal-guide/6.jpg" alt="At the university museum" />
  <img src="/portugal-guide/7.jpg" alt="At the library" />
</div>

<p class="caption-en"><span class="lang-tag">EN</span>I took them through the university museum and the library. The funniest part of the tour was watching Nuno — a wireless communications specialist who, I learned over the week, prefers to listen — patiently translate some of the more poetic Chinese exhibit captions into technical English for Sergio. The museum was half Chinese history, half an unscheduled linguistics seminar.</p>

<p class="caption-zh"><span class="lang-tag">中文</span>带他们看了学校博物馆和图书馆。最好玩的部分是看 Nuno（无线通信专家，这几天我了解到他更喜欢听）给 Sergio 一句一句翻译一些比较诗意的中文展品说明。博物馆参观变成了半节中国史、半场即兴语言课。</p>

---

## 熊猫基地 / The Panda Base

<div class="img-row single">
  <img src="/portugal-guide/9.jpg" alt="At the panda base" />
</div>

<p class="caption-en"><span class="lang-tag">EN</span>Halfway through the week we all went to the panda base. I had been bracing for a slow, polite afternoon and got the opposite. The four of them photographed every single panda they saw. Sergio narrated one panda eating bamboo as if it were a football match. Ricardo asked me, very seriously, whether the red pandas were "a different species or a styling choice". It was, by a wide margin, the loudest I had ever heard Nuno laugh.</p>

<p class="caption-zh"><span class="lang-tag">中文</span>行程中间我们一起去了熊猫基地。我本来准备好迎接一个慢慢走、客客气气的下午，结果完全相反。四个教授把每一只熊猫都拍了下来。Sergio 给一只吃竹子的熊猫做解说，语调像在解说一场球赛。Ricardo 非常认真地问了我一句："小熊猫是另一个物种还是只是染了色？"那次是这一周里我听过 Nuno 最大声的一次笑。</p>

<p class="caption-en"><span class="lang-tag">EN</span>At the end, the four of them sent warm, detailed messages about the visit. I will not quote them — those messages are between us — but I will say that reading them was the moment I realised the week had mattered, not just procedurally, but to actual people. That was a more useful lesson than anything I learned in any seminar that month.</p>

<p class="caption-zh"><span class="lang-tag">中文</span>行程结束的时候，四位教授发来了热情的、详细的反馈。我不在这里引用——那些话属于我们之间——但我想说，看到那些反馈的那一刻我才意识到，这一周不只是流程上完成了，对真人来说也是有意义的。那个月，这是我上过的最重要的一课，比任何 seminar 都重要。</p>

## 六天里我学到的 / What Those Six Days Taught Me

<p class="caption-en"><span class="lang-tag">EN</span>A few small things I did not expect to learn:</p>
<p class="caption-zh"><span class="lang-tag">中文</span>几件原本没计划要学的小事：</p>

<p class="caption-en"><span class="lang-tag">EN</span>— <strong>Portuguese people don't finish dinner until 9 pm.</strong> I had not been warned. I had dinner at 6 pm for the first three days. On day four I adjusted. Everything after dinner is better at 9 pm.<br />— <strong>The pastéis de nata was invented in Lisbon.</strong> This is, depending on who you ask, either a proud national fact or a tourist trap. The professors insisted it is the former.<br />— <strong>Drinks in Europe are cheaper than water.</strong> I do not need to add anything to this.<br />— <strong>Ricardo said, more than once: "Northern Europeans are more organised; Southern Europeans are more flexible."</strong> He was, I think, mostly talking about himself.</p>

<p class="caption-zh"><span class="lang-tag">中文</span>— <strong>葡萄牙人晚上 9 点才吃完饭。</strong>没人提前告诉我。前三天我都是 6 点吃的饭。第四天我调整了。9 点之后的夜确实更好。<br />— <strong>葡式蛋挞起源于里斯本。</strong>这件事——看你问谁——是民族骄傲还是游客陷阱。教授们坚持是前者。<br />— <strong>欧洲饮料比水便宜。</strong>这一条不需要解释。<br />— <strong>Ricardo 不止一次跟我说："北欧人更 organized，南欧人更 flexible。"</strong>他大概有一半在说自己。</p>

## 四个朋友 / Four Friends

<p class="caption-en"><span class="lang-tag">EN</span>The four of them are, briefly:</p>
<p class="caption-zh"><span class="lang-tag">中文</span>四位教授，简短介绍一下：</p>

<p class="caption-en"><span class="lang-tag">EN</span>— <strong>Ricardo</strong> — economist, always smiling, the easiest person in any room to talk to. Carried about half of the visit's social work by himself.<br />— <strong>Pedro</strong> — management professor, looks serious in photos, is in fact the gentlest of the four. Asked me on day three whether I was eating enough.<br />— <strong>Nuno</strong> — wireless communications expert, quiet, reserved, watches more than he speaks. His laugh is rare and worth waiting for.<br />— <strong>Sergio</strong> — ISCTE administrator, the most energetic person in any room he is in. Narrated a panda eating bamboo as if it were a Champions League final.</p>

<p class="caption-zh"><span class="lang-tag">中文</span>— <strong>Ricardo</strong>——经济学教授，永远笑眯眯，和谁都能聊起来。整趟访问的社交工作大概有一半是他一个人完成的。<br />— <strong>Pedro</strong>——管理学教授，拍照时一脸严肃，本人是最温柔的那一个。第三天悄悄问我"有没有吃好"。<br />— <strong>Nuno</strong>——无线通信专家，含蓄内敛，看得多说得少。他笑一次值得等。<br />— <strong>Sergio</strong>——ISCTE 行政人员，精力最旺盛的那一个。把一只熊猫吃竹子解说得像欧冠决赛。</p>

## 关于英文 / On the English

<p class="caption-en"><span class="lang-tag">EN</span>The whole week I forced myself to think in English, not just translate into it. After six days of full-time exposure my ears were noticeably less forgiving of fuzzy English in lectures. I will not pretend I came out the other end native-fluent, but the difference between day one and day six was measurable, and the bar moves because you keep jumping over it.</p>

<p class="caption-zh"><span class="lang-tag">中文</span>这一整周我逼自己用英文思考，不只是把中文翻成英文。六天全天候下来，听 lecture 时对含糊英文的容忍度明显下降了。我不假装自己一周就 native 了，但第一天和第六天的差距是可测量的——跨过那个门槛，靠的就是一直跨。</p>
