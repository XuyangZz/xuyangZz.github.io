## 阿布扎比海洋世界 / Abu Dhabi SeaWorld

<div class="img-row cols-2">
  <img src="/yfel/6.jpg" alt="SeaWorld Abu Dhabi" />
  <img src="/yfel/7.jpg" alt="With a local family at SeaWorld" />
</div>

<p class="caption-en"><span class="lang-tag">EN</span>The first real outing of the trip was to SeaWorld Abu Dhabi. A local family saw me wandering around with my phone out, offered to take photos of me, and ended up taking better photos than I have ever taken of myself. They also sent me all of them within an hour. That was the first time I realised how warm the people here are with strangers.</p>

<p class="caption-zh"><span class="lang-tag">中文</span>这趟旅行第一个真正的外出，是去阿布扎比海洋世界。一个本地家庭看到我一个人举着手机，主动说帮我拍照，最后拍出了我人生中最好的一些照片。一小时内他们把全部照片发给了我。那一刻我才意识到这里的人对陌生人有多热情。</p>

## 一个意大利家庭 / An Italian Family

<div class="img-row single">
  <img src="/yfel/8.jpg" alt="With the Italian family at SeaWorld" />
</div>

<p class="caption-en"><span class="lang-tag">EN</span>We bumped into an Italian family at the aquarium. The father, in his sixties, told me he had been one of the engineers who helped build this very SeaWorld a few years back. He showed me construction photos on his phone, and I showed him photos of robots on mine. We laughed about how the things you spend years building eventually become someone else's weekend.</p>

<p class="caption-zh"><span class="lang-tag">中文</span>在水族馆里偶遇了一个意大利家庭。那位六十多岁的父亲跟我说，他几年前参与了这座海洋世界的建设。他给我翻当时施工时的照片，我给他看手机里的机器人照片。我们都笑了——你花好多年造出来的东西，最后变成了别人周末游玩的地方。</p>

## 法拉利公园 / Ferrari World

<div class="img-row cols-2">
  <img src="/yfel/12.jpg" alt="Ferrari World entrance" />
  <img src="/yfel/13.jpg" alt="Formula Rossa roller coaster" />
</div>

<p class="caption-en"><span class="lang-tag">EN</span>Ferrari World. The headline ride — Formula Rossa — accelerates from 0 to 240 km/h in about two seconds. That is, roughly, the throttle response of a high-end humanoid robot, except applied to your whole body. I cannot honestly tell you what the first half-second felt like. By the time my brain was online again, the ride was already braking.</p>

<p class="caption-zh"><span class="lang-tag">中文</span>法拉利公园。招牌过山车 Formula Rossa 大约两秒从 0 加速到 240 km/h——差不多就是高端人形机器人的油门响应，只不过用在了你全身。诚实说前 0.5 秒我感觉不到什么，等大脑重新上线的时候，列车已经在刹车了。</p>

## WFEL 展览会 / The WFEL Exhibition

<div class="img-row cols-2">
  <img src="/yfel/14.jpg" alt="WFEL exhibition hall" />
  <img src="/yfel/16.jpg" alt="Booths at WFEL" />
</div>

<p class="caption-en"><span class="lang-tag">EN</span>The WFEL exhibition was a window into the engineering culture of the Gulf. University labs, defense vendors, and energy companies all shared one enormous hall. The cross-section of industries in the room was, by itself, a kind of curriculum.</p>

<p class="caption-zh"><span class="lang-tag">中文</span>WFEL 展览会是看海湾地区工程文化的一扇窗。高校实验室、国防承包商、能源公司都挤在一个大厅里。光看现场的行业分布，本身就像一门课。</p>

## 辩论赛评委 / Debate Judge

<div class="img-row cols-2">
  <img src="/yfel/15.jpg" alt="Watching the debate" />
  <img src="/yfel/18.jpg" alt="Debate panel" />
</div>

<p class="caption-en"><span class="lang-tag">EN</span>Khalifa University hosted a debate as part of the WFEL programme, and I was invited to sit on the judging panel alongside faculty from KU and visiting scholars. Listening to students from half a dozen countries argue, in English, about the future of AI in the Gulf — that was a reminder that "international exchange" is less about sightseeing and more about sharing a microphone.</p>

<p class="caption-zh"><span class="lang-tag">中文</span>哈利法大学在 WFEL 期间办了一场辩论赛，我受邀和 KU 的老师、访问学者一起做评委。听着来自五六个国家的学生用英语讨论 AI 在海湾地区的未来——那一刻才真正明白"国际交流"不是看风景，是轮流拿同一个麦克风。</p>

## 中国企业在新能源 / Chinese Companies in New Energy

<div class="img-row cols-2">
  <img src="/yfel/19.jpg" alt="Exhibition booth - Chinese company" />
  <img src="/yfel/20.jpg" alt="Chinese new energy display" />
</div>

<p class="caption-en"><span class="lang-tag">EN</span>Wandering the exhibition floor, I was honestly surprised by how many of the most prominent booths were Chinese. EV batteries, solar panels, grid-scale storage — Chinese companies have a real, visible presence in the Gulf's energy transition. Whatever you think of the geopolitics, the engineering output is hard to argue with.</p>

<p class="caption-zh"><span class="lang-tag">中文</span>在展览会上逛，说实话让我有点意外的是，最显眼的几家展台里中国公司占了很大比例。电动车电池、光伏、电网级储能——中国企业在海湾的能源转型里已经有了实实在在的存在感。撇开地缘政治不谈，技术输出本身很难反驳。</p>

---

## 祷告室 / The Prayer Room

<div class="img-row single">
  <img src="/yfel/21.jpg" alt="Public prayer room" />
</div>

<p class="caption-en"><span class="lang-tag">EN</span>A small but striking cultural detail: in the UAE, public buildings — malls, airports, even some university corridors — have dedicated prayer rooms, and they fill up five times a day on a strict schedule. The first time I saw a building politely come to a brief, organised halt for prayer, I realised how much a society's physical space can reflect what it actually values.</p>

<p class="caption-zh"><span class="lang-tag">中文</span>一个不大但很触动的文化细节：在阿联酋，公共场所——商场、机场、甚至大学的某些走廊——都设有专门的祷告室，每天按时五次整点使用。我第一次看到一栋楼在祷告时间整齐地、有节奏地停下来时，才意识到一个社会的物理空间能多直接地反映它真正看重什么。</p>

---

## 谢赫扎耶德大清真寺 / Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque

<div class="img-row cols-3">
  <img src="/yfel/22.jpg" alt="Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque exterior" />
  <img src="/yfel/23.jpg" alt="Interior arches" />
  <img src="/yfel/24.jpg" alt="Prayer hall" />
</div>

<p class="caption-en"><span class="lang-tag">EN</span>Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque is one of those buildings where the symmetry is so strict it almost feels engineered. I am not religious, but I was honestly moved — the geometry, the way the light comes through, the marble, the sheer patient craftsmanship. The imam told us that during Ramadan, more than ten thousand people pray here in a single night. A building, designed to hold that.</p>

<p class="caption-zh"><span class="lang-tag">中文</span>谢赫扎耶德大清真寺是那种对称严格到近乎被"设计"出来的建筑。我不是信徒，但确实被感动了——几何、光线、大理石、那种耐心的工艺。阿訇跟我们讲，斋月期间一个晚上会有上万人来这里做礼拜。一栋楼，被设计来容纳那种量级的存在。</p>

## 巴基斯坦烤肉 / Pakistani Barbecue

<div class="img-row single">
  <img src="/yfel/25.jpg" alt="Pakistani barbecue with friends" />
</div>

<p class="caption-en"><span class="lang-tag">EN</span>One evening a group of us went out for Pakistani barbecue. The food was smoky, spiced in a way I cannot replicate, and exactly the right amount of messy. We talked for so long the staff started politely wiping the next table behind us. I left smelling like smoke and very happy.</p>

<p class="caption-zh"><span class="lang-tag">中文</span>某天晚上几个人一起去吃了巴基斯坦烤肉。烟熏、香料是那种我复刻不出来的味道，吃得恰到好处地狼狈。我们聊得太久，服务员开始在后面擦下一桌的桌子。我走的时候一身烟味，非常满足。</p>

## 温州超市 / The Wenzhou Supermarket

<div class="img-row cols-2">
  <img src="/yfel/26.jpg" alt="Wenzhou supermarket at night" />
  <img src="/yfel/27.jpg" alt="Inside Wenzhou supermarket" />
</div>

<p class="caption-en"><span class="lang-tag">EN</span>Late one night a few of us ended up at a supermarket in the Wenzhou expat district. The signs were in Chinese, the products were the ones you would see in any Chinese city, and the cashier greeted us in Mandarin. There is something quietly reassuring about being twelve thousand kilometres from home and recognising every brand on the shelf.</p>

<p class="caption-zh"><span class="lang-tag">中文</span>某天深夜我们几个人晃到了温州人聚居区的一家超市。招牌是中文，货是国内常见的那一批，收银员用普通话跟我们打招呼。离家一万两千公里，能认出货架上每一个品牌，那种感觉挺让人安心的。</p>

## 一位约旦朋友 / A Friend from Jordan

<div class="img-row single">
  <img src="/yfel/29.jpg" alt="Jordanian friend" />
</div>

<p class="caption-en"><span class="lang-tag">EN</span>I met a Jordanian engineer on the second day. He is a rocket scientist — the actual, no-modifier kind — and the kind of person you can start talking to about philosophy and end up arguing with about optimal control. I learned more about how engineers think outside my subfield in three dinners with him than in a semester of seminars. I hope we work on something together someday.</p>

<p class="caption-zh"><span class="lang-tag">中文</span>第二天我认识了一位约旦工程师。他是做火箭的——真的那种、不加修饰的火箭科学家——也是那种你可以跟他从哲学聊起、最后吵到最优控制的人。和他吃三顿饭学到的东西，比我一学期听的 seminar 都多。希望将来有一天能一起做点什么。</p>

## 阿布扎比卢浮宫 / Louvre Abu Dhabi

<div class="img-row cols-3">
  <img src="/yfel/32.jpg" alt="Louvre Abu Dhabi dome" />
  <img src="/yfel/35.jpg" alt="Louvre Abu Dhabi gallery" />
  <img src="/yfel/36.jpg" alt="Louvre Abu Dhabi water feature" />
</div>

<p class="caption-en"><span class="lang-tag">EN</span>Jean Nouvel's dome over Louvre Abu Dhabi is one of the most serene pieces of architecture I have ever been inside. The layered, lace-like pattern casts a moving "rain of light" across the floor all day. You stand in a gallery of objects from Mesopotamia to Picasso, and the building itself is quietly arguing that all of these things are part of the same conversation.</p>

<p class="caption-zh"><span class="lang-tag">中文</span>让·努维尔设计的阿布扎比卢浮宫穹顶，是我进过的最安静的建筑之一。那层编织一样的镂空图案，一整天都在地面上投下一场缓缓移动的"光雨"。你站在一个从美索不达米亚到毕加索的展厅里，建筑本身就在悄悄地说：这些东西，全是同一场对话。</p>

## teamLab Phenomena

<div class="img-row cols-2">
  <img src="/yfel/33.jpg" alt="teamLab installation" />
  <img src="/yfel/34.jpg" alt="teamLab light and shadow" />
</div>

<p class="caption-en"><span class="lang-tag">EN</span>teamLab Phenomena is a museum that is also an instrument. Every room reacts to you. You stand still and the room does not care; you move, and the room reorganises itself. I have spent a lot of time thinking about how agents should perceive and act in environments. Standing in teamLab felt like being inside an environment that was actively, gently, perceiving me back.</p>

<p class="caption-zh"><span class="lang-tag">中文</span>teamLab Phenomena 是一个博物馆，也是一件乐器。每一个房间都会对你作出反应。你站着不动，房间不管你；你一动，房间就重新组织自己。我花了很多时间想智能体应该怎么感知环境、怎么行动。站在 teamLab 里像是站在一个也在轻轻反过来感知我的环境里。</p>

## 哈利法塔 / Burj Khalifa

<div class="img-row cols-2">
  <img src="/yfel/37.jpg" alt="Burj Khalifa" />
  <img src="/yfel/38.jpg" alt="At the top of Burj Khalifa" />
</div>

<p class="caption-en"><span class="lang-tag">EN</span>Last stop: Burj Khalifa. The tallest building in the world — and one I had heard about since primary school geography class. Standing at the top, looking down, the cars below look like toys and the desert looks like a flat, soft sea. Not every childhood "I want to see that one day" survives the visit. This one did.</p>

<p class="caption-zh"><span class="lang-tag">中文</span>最后一站：哈利法塔。世界最高建筑——也是我小学地理课上就听说过的地方。站在顶上往下看，底下的车像玩具，沙漠像一片平坦柔软的海。童年的"以后一定要去看看"，并不是每个都能扛过实地打卡。这一次，扛住了。</p>

## 结尾 / Closing

<p class="caption-en"><span class="lang-tag">EN</span>That is the trip, in 38 photos. If you ever get the chance to do a short, fully-funded academic exchange somewhere unexpected — take it. The labs are great. The food is memorable. The people are better.</p>

<p class="caption-zh"><span class="lang-tag">中文</span>这就是这趟旅行，38 张照片讲完。如果有一天你有机会去一个意想不到的地方做一次短的、全额的学术交流——去吧。实验室很棒，食物会记很久，最值的还是人。</p>
